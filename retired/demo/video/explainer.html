The problem

Engineering and procurement are two
spreadsheets that never talk.

Change one engineering requirement, and someone has to manually hunt down which parts are now non-compliant, which suppliers are off the table, and which open POs just broke. That translation is slow, error-prone, and where margin leaks.
The one idea

Separate what never changes from
what's free to vary.

Invariants — the law

A motor must be DC. A housing must meet an ingress-protection floor. These are non-negotiable.

Variables — the freedom

Voltage, frame size, gear ratio. Free to range across an allowed set.
Name those two things, and a standard falls out automatically — derived, not hand-typed. Everything downstream keys off it.
